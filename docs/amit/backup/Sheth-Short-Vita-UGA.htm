Short Vita

Dr. Amit Sheth is an Educator, Entrepreneur, and Researchers.  He is an IEEE Fellow. He enjoys mixing activities that span from conceptual research to entrepreneurship involving commercial products and real-world applications.  He is a Professor of Computer Science and the director of the Large Scale Distributed Information Systems (LSDIS) Lab, at the University of Georgia.  He is also the CTO and co-founder of Semagix, Inc.

Dr. Sheth’s research is in the Database and Information Systems area of the Computer Science.  Because most applications depend on information that is distributed, such as on the Web or in multiple databases in an enterprise, information integration and workflow management for coordination of distributed activities have been at a forefront of research in database and information systems for the last three decades.  Over the last decade, more formal approaches to associate semantics (meaning and understanding) of data and applications have emerged as one of the most vibrant areas of emphasis in the research in this area.  Within this context, Dr. Sheth’s work has primarily concentrated in the following subareas:

  • Heterogeneous Databases: multi-database or federated database systems architecture, schema and view integration, multi-database transactions, multi-database consistency and data quality;
  • Workflow management; Semantic and Autonomic Web Services and Processes: workflow modeling involving heterogeneous user and system tasks; formal workflow scheduling; transactional workflows; dynamic/adaptive and scalable workflow management;
  • Semantic Web including core issues of semantics of information: semantic heterogeneity; comprehensive use of metadata (syntactic, structural, semantic) for digital media (textual and non-textual) integration; semantic proximity and use of content; ontology-driven heterogeneous content integration; multi-ontology query processing; semantic metadata extraction; semantic annotation; semantic enabled networking; semantic graph mining; RDF graph mining; semantic visualization; virtual 3D interfaces on semantic web;
  • Semantic and Autonomic Web Processes: specification of semantic web services (WSDL-S), execution environments, comprehensive role of semantics in Web process encompassing data, function, non-functional semantics and execution; autonomic features for services and processes; semantics enabled light-weight services.

Dr. Sheth has also conducted extensive multi-disciplinary research involving bioinformatics, health care, financial services, homeland and national security and geographic information systems.  He is a faculty member of the Institute of Bioinformatics (UGA), Biomedical Health Science Institute (UGA), Faculty of Engineering (UGA) and UCGIS.

As an Educator

Prof. Sheth jointed the UGA and started the LSDIS lab in 1994. His educational activities have included introduction of innovative courses on Semantic Web, Semantic Web Services & Processes, Semantic Enterprise, Enterprise Integration and Global Information Systems.  These courses combine latest academic research with commercial state of the art research, as well as conceptual frameworks to hands on projects with practical applications.  He was also involved in developing an interdisciplinary program in Masters of Internet Technology and has been on the steering committee of New Media Institute at UGA.   His favorite accomplishment is the UGA Career Center Recognition/Award for “greatly contributing to the career development of UGA students.” His guiding philosophy for advising graduate advisees is "learning how to learn."  Additional thoughts on teaching appears in this 2003 interview.

As an Entrepreneur

In August 1999, Sheth founded Taalee, Inc., a Venture Capital funded enterprise software and internet infrastructure startup based on the technology developed at the LSDIS lab.  He managed Taalee as its CEO until June 2001.  After Taalee was acquired by Voquette, Inc and subsequently merged with Protégé Holdings to become Semangix, Inc., he has been the CTO/co-Founder for Voquette, Inc. and Semagix. He has also founded another high-tech company—Infocosm, Inc, which commercialized workflow technology. He has provided consulting to a wide variety of companies involving real world solutions to the real world problems. His research has led to several commercial products and applications (including AdaptX/Harness from Bellcore based on InfoHarness project, METEOR EAppS from Infocosm based on the METEOR project, and Freedom from Semagix or Semantic Engine from Taalee based on SCORE technology and VideoAnywhere project), as well as two patents (6,311,194, 5,241,675). From 1985 to 1994, he served in R&D groups at Bellcore (now Telcordia Technologies), Unisys, and Honeywell.   

As a Researcher

Prof. Sheth has given twenty two keynote talks at international conferences and meetings, as part of over 175 invited talks, tutorials and professional courses. He has over 200 publications, including two outstanding conference papers, and some of the most cited papers in federated databases, workflow management and semantic interoperability. This includes some of most cited paper in the DB/IS (at least 2500 citations excluding self citations), and related areas in computer science (e.g., Sheth and Larson paper is the most cited Database/Information Systems paper, it also the all-time most cited paper on information integration, the second most cited distributed database paper and one of the five most cited database paper ever in CiteSeer database; about ten of his publications have over 100 citations and additional 14 have over 50 citations as recorded on Google Scholar). He is on the editorial board of five journals, and the Editor-in-Chief of the International Journal on Semantic Web and Information Systems. With Ramesh Jain, he recently started a Kluwer book series on "Semantic Web and Beyond: Computing for Human Experience."  His views related to his current research interest appear in this 2004 interview.

Research he has led has been funded by most major funding agencies ncluding NSF, NIH, NIST, DARPA, ARDA, NRL, as well as several companies.  Projects he has led at UGA have been funded over $5M.  Overall, he has received over $11 million in research funds (including research led at Honeywell/Unisys/Bellcore); and over $16 million including funding that supported entrepreneurial efforts he has led or started.

During 2004, he served as a Program co-chair/Co-organizer of.  Among over twenty conferences/workshops that he has (co-)chaired are the IEEE International Conference on Services Computing, Dagstuhl Workshop on Semantic Interoperability, NSF-EU Workshop on DB/IS Issues for Semantic Web and Enterprises, IEEE Digital Library Conference and WWW10 Workshop on Semantic Web.  In 2004, he received IBM Faculty Award and IBM Eclipse Innovation Grants award.  Earlier, he served twice as an ACM Lecturer, was one of the two international advisors of Japan's TARA program, and has served on over 110 program and organization committees.  He received his B.E.(Hons.,1981) from B.I.T.S., Pilani, India and M.S.(1983) and Ph.D.(1985) from the Ohio State University.

The LSDIS lab (http://lsdis.cs.uga.edu) is one of the top research labs in its area of research in the world.  Semagix which has commercialized his research is the leading company with the most comprehensive semantic technologies for the enterprises.
