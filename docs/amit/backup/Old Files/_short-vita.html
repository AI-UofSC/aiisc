Short Vita

Dr. Amit Sheth directs the Large Scale Distributed Information Systems (LSDIS) Lab, is an Associate Professor of Computer Science at the
University of Georgia, and an Adjunct Associate Professor in the College of Computing at the Georgia Institute of Technology.  Earlier
he worked for nine years in the R&D labs at Bellcore, Unisys, and Honeywell.  His primary current research interests include
multiparadigm transactional workflow (project METEOR), management of heterogeneous digital data and semantic issues in global information
systems (project InfoQuilt), telemedicine/teleconsulting, and electronic commerce.

He has led projects on heterogeneous DDBMS, factory information system, integration of AI-database systems (BrAID), transactional
workflows (PROMT and METEOR), federated database tools (BERDI and TAILOR), multidatabase consistency, and data quality (Q-Data).  LSDIS
lab (http://www.cs.uga.edu/LSDIS) maintains very active collaboration with industry, and has been awarded four significant projects in the
areas of global information system to support heterogeneous digital media management, workflow management, and teleconsulting/telemedicine.  Its nearly $1.8 million in R&D funds support three full time staff and approx. ten research assistants.
 

Prof. Sheth has approx. 100 publications to his credit, given over 50 invited and colloquia talks and 15 tutorials and professional courses,
and lead for international conferences and a workshop as a General/Program (Co-)Chair in the area of information system
cooperation/interoperability, workflow management, and parallel and distributed information systems.  He is a founding member
and executive committee member of the Intl. Foundation on Cooperative Information Systems.  He has also served twice as an ACM Lecturer, has
served on over thirty five program and organization committees, and is on the editorial board of five journals.
