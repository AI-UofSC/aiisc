Multiparadigm Transactional Workflow:
        Technology and its Application in Efficient Healthcare Delivery
 
        Amit Sheth
        Large Scale Distributed Information Systems Laboratory
        University of Georgia
        http://lsdis.cs.uga.edu/

Recent trends, especially the move towards managed care, has put significant additional demands on Healthcare businesses.  Key among
these are support for internal auditing and compulsory performance reporting, and reducing the cost of delivery, while at the same time
maintaining or reducing the cost of operations and improving the quality of care.  An important requirement for meeting these needs is
to significantly improve the processes through coordination of multiple personnel and information systems that are spread over
multiple organizations in an enterprise as well as across multiple enterprises.  A good workflow technology has the potential to make
this happen.

However, in spite of significant hype, current workflow products generally fall short in meeting requirements of complex applications.
One of the key objectives of the Healthcare Information Infrastructure Technology (HIIT) project is to develop a workflow automation
technology that is suitable for the needs of healthcare industry, and more importantly, demonstrate its use.  In HIIT, the Large Scale
Distributed Information Systems Lab at the University of Georgia (UGA) is pushing the state-of-the-art in the workflow automation technology
by supporting two key features:

(a) supporting high-level graphical design of workflows involving both human and computerized activities,
and their mostly automatic translation to executable code, and
(b) ability to support realistic environments consisting of multiple infrastructures involving internet/World-Wide-Web, distributed object
management (CORBA), Lotus Notes, and wireless technologies, with appropriate support for recovery, failure-handling, auditing, data
consistency, and security.

This presentation discusses the Immunization Tracking application and the role of UGA's workflow technology in an application partnership in
the Healthcare Enterprise Information Management spoke of HIIT.  This partnership involving Connecticut Healthcare Education and Research
Foundation (CHREF), UGA, and Microelectronics and Computer Technology Corporation (MCC).  The clinical aspects of the application involves
creating and maintaining immunization records for children in hospitals or clinics and updating centralized patient records.  The tracking aspect involves using distributed and heterogeneous databases to generate reminders for parents, and to generate reports for use by state agencies, Medicaid office, and case workers.  The system design is extensible with respect to additional healthcare functions, participation by additional organizations, and the use of a variety of
technologies and standards.  Additional information for this work including publications and presentations can be found at
http://lsdis.cs.uga.edu/proj/proj.html  by sending email to: amit@cs.uga.edu.

Dr. Amit Sheth directs the Large Scale Distributed Information Systems (LSDIS) Lab, is an Associate Professor of Computer Science at the
University of Georgia, and an Adjunct Associate Professor in the College of Computing at the Georgia Institute of Technology.  Earlier
he worked for nine years in the R&D labs at Bellcore, Unisys, and Honeywell.  His primary current research interests include transactional
workflow (project METEOR), management of heterogeneous digital data and semantic issues in global information systems (project InfoQuilt),
especially the use of ontologies, contexts and metadata, and semantic relationships across heterogeneous digital data repositories.

He has led projects on heterogeneous DDBMS, factory information system, integration of AI-database systems (BrAID), transactional
workflows (PROMT and METEOR), federated database tools (BERDI and TAILOR), multidatabase consistency, and data quality (Q-Data).  LSDIS
lab (http://www.cs.uga.edu/LSDIS) maintains very active collaboration with industry, and has won significant funded projects in the areas of
interoperable information system and workflow management (under the Healthcare Information Infrastructure Program awarded by NIST) and
the InfoHarness system for scalability search of heterogeneous information (awarded in the Massive Digital Data Systems initiative).

Prof. Sheth has published over 80 papers, given over 50 invited and colloquia talks and 14 tutorials, and lead two international
conferences and a workshop as a General/Program (Co-)Chair. Currently, he is a General (Co-)Chair of the 1st Intl. Conference on
Interoperable and Cooperative Information Systems and the Program Chair of the NSF Workshop on Workflow and Process Automation.  He is a
founding member and executive committee member of the Intl. Foundation on Cooperative Information Systems.  He has also served twice as an
ACM Lecturer, has served on over thirty program and organization committees, and is on the editorial board of five journals.
